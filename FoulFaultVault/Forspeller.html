Forspeller

Version 5.4

Welcome to the Forspeller; where letters are assumed to be atoms, carrying a specific mass and charge, consonants function as stable concepts, and vowals behave as dynamic operaters to com-bine them.

This 'Forspeller' is based on the idea that words carry mass and explores the chemistry behind it. It uses a given set of concepts per letter, and rules for parsing. Both created after multiple testing, however, still in development.

Keep in mind: The goal is not to fit the theory to a given meaning, rather to create a different way of looking at things.

🔹 Consonants (primitives)

A=action/initiate, B=barrier/bound, C=create, D=direction, E=exit/exterior, F=flow/force, G=gather/ground, H=hold/hide, I=inward/self, J=join, K=kinetic/cut, L=layer/level, M=motion, N=negation/not, O=orbit/cycle, P=push, Q=query, R=repeat/rotate, S=separate/split, T=terminus/tool, U=under/container, V=void, W=wave/water (or UU if toggled), X=cross/negation, Y=branch/why, Z=zero/null

🔸 Vowel operators

A = binary: left initiates right ; unary: left initiates (action from left)
E = binary: left exits to right ; unary: left exits (outward flow)
I = binary: swap left & right ; unary: left becomes self‑referential (inward)
O = binary: repeat left once, then right ; unary: repeat left once
U = binary: unify left & right ; unary: left becomes container (reflexive)
Initial vowel acts on implicit I (self). No vowel is ignored.

📌 Special rules

• Emotions → implicit I prefix
• "am" before emotion → injects M (motion)
• Pronouns: I→I, YOU→U, HE/SHE/IT→H
• Function words (a, an, the, is, are, be, of, to, for, and, or, but) → inert (quoted)

Every vowel is active: binary (C V C) or unary (C V).
